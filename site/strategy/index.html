The "Digital Tenant" Trap

You Are Renting Your Business Infrastructure

Microsoft 365. Google Workspace. Salesforce. Slack. Dropbox. None of these tools belong to you. You are a digital tenant.

Unpredictable Costs

You pay monthly rent for every user. Prices rise annually. You have no control over the cost of your own operations.

Forced Updates

Features change, buttons move, and workflows break without your consent. Your team relearns tools on someone else's schedule.

Data Jurisdiction

Your sensitive data sits on servers in jurisdictions you didn't choose, governed by terms you didn't negotiate.

Vendor Lock-In

Try exporting your data. You'll find proprietary formats, missing metadata, and switching costs engineered to keep you trapped.

The alternative is becoming a digital owner - running open-source software on infrastructure you control.

Most Data Is Invisible to AI

Your business generates valuable knowledge, but it's locked in formats machines can't understand

The organizations that thrive in the coming decade will be those that treat information as infrastructure. Currently, most businesses suffer from "dark data":

Unstructured Chaos

Critical procedures buried in PDFs nobody reads. Knowledge trapped in documents that are just pixels to a computer.

Conflicting Truths

Excel sheets that contradict each other, saved on different desktops. No single source of truth. Which version is correct?

Dead Archives

Email threads containing vital decisions that disappear into the void. Institutional knowledge that leaves when employees do.

The AI Reality: AI requires structured, machine-readable data. If your knowledge isn't structured, it cannot be leveraged. You cannot build a "second brain" for your company on a foundation of disorganized files.
